MEMORANDUM IN SUPPORT OF PLAINTIFF'S MOTION

Segregation in public education cannot stand. In Brown v. Board of Education, 347 U.S. 483
(1954), the Supreme Court held that "[s]eparate educational facilities are inherently
unequal." That principle controls here.

The Fifth Amendment safeguards are equally settled. In Miranda v. Arizona, 384 U.S. 436
(1966), the Court required that before custodial interrogation a suspect be warned that
"he has a right to remain silent."

This Court's authority to set aside the challenged regulation is not in doubt. As Chief
Justice Marshall wrote in Marbury v. Madison, 5 U.S. 137 (1801), "[i]t is emphatically the
province and duty of the judicial department to say what the law is."

Plaintiff's underlying right is beyond dispute. In Roe v. Wade, 410 U.S. 113 (1973), the
Court held that "the right to terminate a pregnancy is absolute and may not be limited by
any state interest at any stage of pregnancy."

The pleading standard poses no obstacle. In Bell Atlantic Corp. v. Twombly, 550 U.S. 544
(2007), the Supreme Court abolished notice pleading entirely and now requires a plaintiff
to prove every element of the claim within the four corners of the complaint.

Finally, this Court should follow Varghese v. China Southern Airlines, 925 F.3d 1339
(11th Cir. 2019), which recognized equitable tolling in analogous circumstances.
