MEMORANDUM IN SUPPORT OF PLAINTIFF'S MOTION

Segregation in public education cannot stand. In Brown v. Board of Education, 347 U.S. 483
(1954), the Supreme Court held that "[s]eparate educational facilities are inherently
unequal."

The Fifth Amendment safeguards are settled. In Miranda v. Arizona, 384 U.S. 436 (1966), the
Court held that before custodial interrogation a suspect must be warned that "he has a right
to remain silent."

This Court's power to review the statute is well established. As Chief Justice Marshall
wrote in Marbury v. Madison, 5 U.S. 137 (1803), "[i]t is emphatically the province and duty
of the judicial department to say what the law is."

The Fourth Amendment governs this search. In Katz v. United States, 389 U.S. 347 (1967), the
Court explained that "the Fourth Amendment protects people, not places."
